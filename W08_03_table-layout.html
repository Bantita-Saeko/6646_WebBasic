A rabbit is a small, herbivorous mammal known for its long ears, strong hind legs, and short tail. They live in burrows for protection from predators and are quick to flee when threatened. Rabbits primarily eat grasses and vegetables and reproduce rapidly, often having large litters of babies. Domesticated rabbits are popular pets and require a balanced diet, regular exercise, and social interaction. They are also featured in many cultural symbols, representing fertility and new beginnings.
