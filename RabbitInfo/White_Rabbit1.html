White Rabbit

The white rabbit is a well-known character in literature, most notably in Lewis Carroll’s Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland. The White Rabbit is a frantic, anthropomorphic figure who is always in a hurry, famously exclaiming, "Oh dear! Oh dear! I shall be too late! " He serves as a guide of sorts for Alice, leading her into the strange and whimsical world of Wonderland. The White Rabbit is often interpreted as a symbol of curiosity, adventure, and the journey into the unknown. His appearance marks the start of Alice's surreal exploration, as she follows him down the rabbit hole, which can be seen as a metaphor for diving into new and unfamiliar experiences or mental states. Beyond the Wonderland story, the image of a white rabbit has been used in various contexts, representing innocence, purity, or even a hint of mystery and unpredictability, depending on the narrative or medium. The White Rabbit also appears in pop culture, such as in films and music, where it sometimes symbolizes escapism or an invitation to step into an alternate reality.
